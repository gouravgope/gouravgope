Several Indian scientists and engineers, particularly those working in the nuclear and space sectors, have died in unnatural or unexplained circumstances after 1947.
These deaths have sparked widespread speculation, conspiracy theories, and allegations of international intelligence involvement to sabotage India's technical 
advancements. Below is a list of prominent Indian scientists and engineers whose deaths are considered mysterious or suspicious based on various reports: 
--------------
### Nuclear Scientists & Engineers (BARC, IGCAR, NPCIL)
1. Homi Jehangir Bhabha (1966): The "Father of Indian Nuclear Program" died in an Air India crash near Mont Blanc, leading to theories of CIA sabotage.
2. Lokanathan Mahalingam (2009): Kaiga Atomic Power Station scientist, found dead in the Kali River after disappearing while walking.
3. Ravi Mule (2004): NPCIL employee, death deemed murder with concerns over the investigation.
4. Umang Singh & Partha Pratim Bag (2009): Two BARC researchers killed in a mysterious lab fire.
5. Mahadevan Padmanabhan Iyer (2010): BARC engineer found dead in his home; reports suggested a "professional murder".
6. S. Ananthanarayanan (2010): IGCAR scientist found dead on a railway track in Chennai.
7. Dalia Nayek (2009): Saha Institute researcher, died from alleged mercury poisoning.
8. Uma Rao (2011): Retired BARC scientist, death challenged as suicide by family.
10. Mohammad Mustafa (2012): 24-year-old IGCAR scientist found dead in his quarters.
11. K.K. Joshi & Abhish Shivam (2013): INS Arihant nuclear submarine engineers found dead near Vizag Naval Yard.
12. Akshay P Chavan (2010): BARC employee found dead after an alleged fall from his balcony.
---------------
### Space and Aerospace Engineers 
Vikram Sarabhai (1971): "Father of India's Space Program," found dead in a hotel room in Kerala; no autopsy was performed.
G.K. Kumaravel: Arjun tank developer, died in a car crash.
Suresh Kumar (2019): ISRO scientist found murdered in his apartment.
