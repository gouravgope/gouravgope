Several scientists and engineers, particularly those in sensitive defense or AI fields, have died in circumstances that raised public suspicion, with the GEC-Marconi 
incident involving 25 deaths between 1982–1990 being the most prominent, though most were ruled suicides or accidents. Another recent case includes a 2024 
AI researcher death. 

1. Suchir Balaji (2024): A 26-year-old in San Francisco involved in AI research and potential whistleblowing against OpenAI was found dead from a self-inflicted
   gunshot wound, though his status attracted public interest.
2. GEC-Marconi Scientist Deaths (1982–1990): While mostly British-based, many were linked to US Strategic Defense Initiative projects. Deaths included unconventional
   suicides (e.g., car exhaust/carbon monoxide poisoning, driving into a building).
4. Dr. John V. H. Lewis (1987): A researcher for Marconi who died by suicide via carbon monoxide poisoning in his garage.
5. Jonathan Walsh (1987): A digital communications expert for Marconi who died in a hotel fire. 
--------------------
Note: In many cases involving suspicious deaths of researchers, authorities officially ruled them as suicides or accidents despite surrounding speculation.
